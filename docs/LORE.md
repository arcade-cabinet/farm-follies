# Farm Follies — Lore

## Setting

A family farm on a Monday morning. A tornado has appeared without warning and is making its way across the property, scooping up every barnyard animal in its path and flinging them skyward.

## The Farmer

The player character is an unnamed farmer caught outside when the tornado hit. Rather than fleeing, they decided to try to catch the animals before they scatter across the county. This is either heroic or deeply unwise — probably both.

The farmer is controlled by the player, moving left and right at the bottom of the screen, catching animals that fall from the tornado above.

## The Animals

Nine types of barnyard animals are swept up in the tornado. Each has a normal variant and a special variant with a distinct personality.

### Chicken
Common, fast-falling. The golden variant (Golden Cluck) is particularly excitable and drops explosive eggs when tapped.

### Duck
Medium fall speed. Sir Quacksalot, the blue duck, is theatrical — upon activation, floats down on a cascade of feathers at a dramatically reduced speed.

### Pig
Hefty, medium fall speed. Mudslide, the pink pig, creates a mud zone on activation that slows nearby falling animals.

### Sheep
Light, gentle fall. Shadow Wool, the black sheep, wraps the entire stack in protective wool — temporary invincibility.

### Goat
Nimble, medium fall. Gruff, the gray goat, bleats loudly on activation, stunning nearby falling animals momentarily.

### Cow
Heavy, slower fall. Muddy Bessie, the brown cow, is the most mechanically important special variant — she shoots poop projectiles that grow bouncy bushes where they land, giving missed animals a second chance.

### Goose
Medium weight. Mother Goose, the golden goose, creates a sticky golden landing zone that centers incoming animals and reduces wobble impact.

### Rooster
Heavy, proud. The Dawn Caller, an albino rooster, crows loudly on activation — the sound attracts falling animals toward the center of the screen.

### Horse
Heaviest animal. The Haymaker, a tan horse, summons floating hay platforms that animals can bounce on, creating additional catch opportunities.

## The Tornado

The tornado is not malevolent — it's just a tornado. It moves back and forth across the top of the screen, dropping animals at intervals. As time passes and levels increase, it moves faster and drops animals more frequently. It is the player's primary environmental adversary.

The tornado has a funnel that visually emits each animal. It carries debris (leaves, hay, miscellaneous farm objects) that orbit the funnel.

## The Stakes

Technically none — this is a casual arcade game. Thematically, the farmer is trying to save the animals from being flung across the county. Banking is the act of getting a stack of animals into the barn (the bank zone) safely. Toppling means the stack scattered.

## Tone

Cheerful chaos. The situation is absurd and the farmer knows it. The sound design and animal expressions lean into the comedy. Nothing is grim. Even losing a life is a pratfall, not a tragedy.
